About

I'm a Concurrent Master's student in Integrative Biology, Computer Science, and Applied Mathematics at Harvard University, both in OEB and in SEAS. Most of my research has been in climate- and conservation-related data science, which has informed my current goal of understanding and communicating equity in social impact data science problems. I am especially interested in taking an ML foundations and computational neuroscience approach toward social data science problems. Specifically, I hope to combine neural representation engineering with a variety of ML-related visualization techniques to communicate embedded inequities in large model decision-making.
